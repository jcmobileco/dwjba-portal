Oversight & Accountability

Leadership &
Governance

The DWJBA is governed by a bi-jurisdictional board composed of appointed representatives from both the City of Detroit and Wayne County. This unique structure ensures balanced oversight, shared fiscal responsibility, and full public transparency.

Board meetings are open to the public in accordance with Michigan's Open Meetings Act. Agendas, minutes, and financial documents are available through our Documents portal.

Our History

A Legacy of
Public Stewardship

From an iconic civic landmark to a digital transformation — seven decades of serving Detroit and Southeast Michigan.

1955
Coleman A. Young Municipal Center Opens

Designed by Harley, Ellington & Day — Detroit's primary government complex opens with 780,000 sq ft in twin towers: a 14-floor Admin Tower and 20-floor Courts Tower.

Founding Milestone
1958
Spirit of Detroit Dedicated

Marshall Fredericks' iconic 26-foot bronze sculpture is dedicated July 26, 1958 — becoming Detroit's most beloved civic landmark and an enduring symbol of the city's spirit and resilience.

Cultural Landmark
1990s
DWJBA Formally Established

The Detroit-Wayne Joint Building Authority is formally constituted as a bi-jurisdictional public body — creating a unified governance structure for managing shared civic facilities across City and County.

Governance
2005
Hines Professional Management Partnership

DWJBA partners with Hines, a world-class Fortune 500 property management firm — elevating building operations to the highest professional standards and earning the first of three consecutive EPA Energy Star certifications.

3× Energy Star
2018
BOMA TOBY Award for Excellence

The Building Owners and Managers Association recognizes the Coleman A. Young Municipal Center with its prestigious The Outstanding Building of the Year (TOBY) Award — validating world-class operational standards.

Industry Award
2024
Digital Transformation & AI Initiative

Under CEO Clarinda Barnett-Harrison, DWJBA launches a comprehensive modernization initiative — introducing AI-powered visitor navigation, digital services, upgraded capital infrastructure, and expanded public transparency programs.
