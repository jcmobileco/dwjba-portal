The Building

The Coleman A. Young Municipal Center stands as Detroit's premier civic landmark. Completed in 1955 and designed by the firm of Harley, Ellington & Day, the 19-story tower anchors the southern terminus of Woodward Avenue — the city's principal boulevard and the nation's first paved highway. Its mid-century modernist design reflects the optimism of postwar Detroit, with clean limestone facades, a commanding river-facing presence, and interiors built to project institutional permanence.

Today, the CAYMC remains the operational core of Detroit's government. It houses the Mayor's Office, Detroit City Council chambers, the 36th District Court, the City Clerk, and dozens of departments that deliver essential services — from property assessments and building permits to public safety and human services. The building also serves as a civic gathering point, welcoming over 500,000 residents and visitors annually through its doors for court proceedings, municipal services, public hearings, and community events.

Security & Access

All visitors must pass through security screening at building entry points. Valid government-issued photo ID is required.

No weapons, sharp objects, or prohibited items. Bags and personal items are subject to X-ray screening.

36th District Court

Court hours: 8:00 AM – 4:30 PM
Traffic, criminal, and civil divisions

Arrive at least 30 minutes early for scheduled hearings. Court calendars available at 36thdistrictcourt.org

Parking
