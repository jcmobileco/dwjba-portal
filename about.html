About the Detroit-Wayne Joint
Building Authority

A bi-jurisdictional public authority dedicated to managing and operating critical civic infrastructure for the City of Detroit and Wayne County since 1924.

A civic institution built to serve.

The Detroit-Wayne Joint Building Authority (DWJBA) is a bi-jurisdictional public authority responsible for managing and operating critical civic facilities serving the City of Detroit and Wayne County. Established through a joint agreement between Detroit's municipal government and Wayne County, the DWJBA oversees the Coleman A. Young Municipal Center — one of Michigan's most prominent government complexes — along with Spirit of Detroit Plaza and surrounding public spaces.

Our mandate is to ensure that the buildings housing Detroit's courts, city and county offices, and essential public services remain safe, accessible, and operationally excellent. From daily building operations and safety programs to major capital improvement projects, the DWJBA serves as the institutional backbone of Detroit's civic center.

As stewards of public trust, we maintain the highest standards of transparency, fiscal responsibility, and operational integrity — delivering value to every resident, tenant, and visitor who depends on these facilities each day.

Manage. Maintain.
Serve Detroit.

To manage, maintain, and improve the public facilities entrusted to the DWJBA — ensuring safe, efficient, and welcoming spaces that enable city and county government to serve Detroit's residents and businesses with excellence.

02 Our Vision

A National Model
for Civic Excellence.

To be a national model for bi-jurisdictional civic facility management — recognized for operational excellence, sustainable stewardship, and a deep commitment to the communities and institutions we serve in Southeast Michigan.

03 Core Responsibility

Accountable to Both
City & County.

As a joint body of the City of Detroit and Wayne County, the DWJBA bears statutory responsibility for the physical management, security, procurement oversight, and long-range capital planning of all facilities — accountable to both governments and the public they represent.

Leadership & Governance

The DWJBA is governed by a bi-jurisdictional board of appointed representatives from the City of Detroit and Wayne County — ensuring balanced oversight, shared fiscal responsibility, and full public transparency. Board meetings are open to the public per Michigan's Open Meetings Act.
