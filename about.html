Architectural Perspective

Two towers.
One mission.

The Municipal Center comprises two interconnected towers rising above Woodward Avenue — the 14-floor Administration Tower housing city and county executive offices, and the 20-floor Courts Tower serving the 3rd Judicial Circuit Court and 36th District Court. Together, they form the largest civic complex in Michigan, purpose-built to unite government under one roof.

The Spirit
of Detroit

Standing 26 feet tall on the Woodward Avenue plaza, Marshall Fredericks' 1958 bronze masterpiece is one of the most recognized public artworks in America. The seated figure holds a gilded sphere representing God in the left hand and a family group in the right — symbolizing the relationship between the divine and the human family. The Spirit of Detroit plaza hosts civic celebrations, jersey dressings for Detroit sports championships, and is the most photographed landmark in the city.

Managed by DWJBA

The Detroit-Wayne Joint Building Authority oversees all operations, maintenance, and capital improvements for the Coleman A. Young Municipal Center — ensuring world-class facility management for the public buildings that serve Detroit and Wayne County.

Property Management

Hines, a global real estate leader, has managed CAYMC operations since 2005 — delivering institutional-grade facility services to one of Michigan's most complex public buildings.

Energy & Sustainability

Three consecutive EPA Energy Star certifications and a BOMA TOBY Award recognize CAYMC's commitment to energy efficiency and sustainable building operations.

Security & Access

24/7 security operations, visitor screening, access control systems, and emergency management protocols protect thousands of daily building occupants and visitors.

Capital Projects

Ongoing modernization and infrastructure improvements — from elevator systems and HVAC upgrades to ADA compliance and life-safety enhancements across both towers.
