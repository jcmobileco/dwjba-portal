About the Authority

Since 1955, the Detroit-Wayne Joint Building Authority has served as the bi-jurisdictional steward of Detroit's most critical civic infrastructure. As a public body jointly created by the City of Detroit and Wayne County, the DWJBA is responsible for managing, maintaining, and modernizing the landmark facilities that house government services, judicial operations, and public programs — serving hundreds of thousands of residents and visitors each year.

The Coleman A. Young Municipal Center stands as the architectural centerpiece of this mandate: a 670,000-square-foot complex at the foot of Woodward Avenue, flanked by the Detroit River and the city's resurgent downtown core. Since its completion in 1955, the building has anchored Detroit's civic life, housing the offices of the Mayor, City Council, 36th District Court, and dozens of essential departments that serve Wayne County's 1.7 million residents.

An unrivaled building at the foot of Woodward Avenue, anchoring Detroit's civic heart

Bordered by the Detroit River to the south and the city's resurgent downtown core, the Coleman A. Young Municipal Center stands at the terminus of Woodward Avenue — Detroit's principal boulevard and the nation's first paved highway. The building commands a presence that reflects the authority and permanence of the institutions it houses.
