About The Authority

Since 1955, the Detroit-Wayne Joint Building Authority has served as the bi-jurisdictional steward of Detroit's most critical civic infrastructure. As a public body jointly created by the City of Detroit and Wayne County, the DWJBA is responsible for managing, maintaining, and modernizing the landmark facilities that house government services, judicial operations, and public programs — serving hundreds of thousands of residents and visitors each year.

From the Coleman A. Young Municipal Center — one of the most architecturally significant government complexes in the Midwest — to the iconic Spirit of Detroit Plaza, the DWJBA operates at the intersection of civic excellence, public accountability, and community pride. Our mandate is simple: ensure that the spaces serving Detroit's government and its people are safe, efficient, and world-class.

Oversight & Accountability

Leadership &
Governance

The DWJBA is governed by a bi-jurisdictional board composed of appointed representatives from both the City of Detroit and Wayne County. This unique structure ensures balanced oversight, shared fiscal responsibility, and full public transparency.

Board meetings are open to the public in accordance with Michigan's Open Meetings Act. Agendas, minutes, and financial documents are available through our Documents portal.

Our History

A Legacy of
Public Stewardship

From an iconic civic landmark to a digital transformation — seven decades of serving Detroit and Southeast Michigan.

1955
Coleman A. Young Municipal Center Opens

Detroit's primary government complex opens with 780,000 sq ft in twin towers — 14-floor Admin Tower and 20-floor Courts Tower, designed by Harley, Ellington & Day.

Founding Milestone
1958
Spirit of Detroit Dedicated

Marshall Fredericks' iconic 26-foot bronze sculpture is dedicated July 26, 1958 — becoming Detroit's most beloved civic landmark and a symbol of the city's resilience.

Cultural Landmark
1990s
DWJBA Formally Established

The Detroit-Wayne Joint Building Authority is formally constituted as a bi-jurisdictional public body — creating unified governance for shared civic facilities across City and County.

Governance
2005
Hines Professional Management Partnership

DWJBA partners with Hines, a Fortune 500 property management firm — elevating operations to world-class standards and earning the first of three consecutive EPA Energy Star certifications.
