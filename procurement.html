Procurement & Vendor Opportunities

The Detroit-Wayne Joint Building Authority is committed to transparent, competitive procurement that creates opportunities for businesses of all sizes — with special outreach to minority-owned, women-owned, and Detroit-based enterprises.

Getting Started

How to Do Business With DWJBA

Follow these four steps to participate in the Authority's competitive procurement process. All solicitations are publicly posted through the Michigan Intergovernmental Trade Network (MITN) powered by BidNet Direct.

  1. 01

    Register as a Vendor

    Create a free account on BidNet Direct / MITN (Michigan Intergovernmental Trade Network). Registration allows you to receive automatic bid notifications, download solicitation documents, and submit electronic responses for opportunities matching your business categories.

  2. 02

    Review Opportunities

    Monitor posted RFPs (Requests for Proposals), IFBs (Invitations for Bids), and other procurement notices. Read each solicitation document carefully — including scope, technical specifications, insurance requirements, and submission instructions before preparing your response.

  3. 03

    Submit Your Response

    Follow all submission requirements and deadlines exactly as stated in the solicitation. Late or non-conforming submissions cannot be accepted. Contact the DWJBA Procurement Office with questions during the designated Q&A window prior to each deadline.

  4. 04

    Award & Contract

    Responses are evaluated by an internal review committee against the criteria outlined in each solicitation. Qualified firms are selected, and award notices are posted publicly. Successful vendors will be contacted to begin contract negotiations and execution.

Resources for Vendors

Vendor Resources

The DWJBA is committed to fostering an inclusive vendor community. We actively encourage participation from minority-owned, women-owned, and Detroit-based businesses in all procurement activities.

MBE / WBE Certification

The Authority recognizes and encourages certified Minority Business Enterprises (MBE) and Women Business Enterprises (WBE). Relevant certifications should be included with your bid submission. Certification through the Michigan Unified Certification Program (MUCP) or comparable agency is accepted.

Detroit-Based Business Priority

In accordance with the City of Detroit's local purchasing policies, the DWJBA gives priority consideration to Detroit-based businesses where applicable under state procurement law. Firms with a principal place of business within the City of Detroit should indicate this in their submission.

BidNet Direct / MITN

All DWJBA solicitations are posted through BidNet Direct on the Michigan Intergovernmental Trade Network (MITN). A free vendor account provides access to all open solicitations, addenda, Q&A responses, and bid results. Registration is required to submit a formal response.

Insurance Requirements

All vendors awarded contracts must carry appropriate insurance coverage including general liability, workers' compensation, and professional liability where applicable. Specific requirements are detailed in each solicitation. The DWJBA must be named as an additional insured on all policies.
