About the Landmark

The Spirit of Detroit stands as one of America's most beloved public sculptures — a monumental 26-foot bronze figure positioned at the entrance of the Coleman A. Young Municipal Center at 2 Woodward Avenue. For more than six decades, the Spirit has been a symbol of resilience, civic pride, and the enduring strength of Detroit and its people.

Sculpted by renowned artist Marshall Fredericks and dedicated July 26, 1958, the Spirit of Detroit has become the city's most recognizable symbol. The figure is depicted seated, holding a gilt sphere representing God in one hand and a family group in the other — a powerful statement of community, faith, and shared humanity. The Latin inscription reads: "Now the Lord is that Spirit: and where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is Liberty."

From championship celebrations to public health campaigns, the Spirit of Detroit participates in the full life of the city. Through custom jerseys, illuminated displays, and community gatherings at its base, the sculpture remains a living landmark — not merely a monument, but an active participant in Detroit's ongoing story.

DWJBA stewards this landmark, ensuring it remains a vibrant center of civic pride and public engagement. The Authority manages all requests for jersey programs, special events, and ceremonial use of the Spirit of Detroit site.

The Jersey Program

Dress the Spirit of Detroit

Organizations, sports teams, and community groups request custom jerseys to honor milestones, celebrate victories, and mark cultural moments. The Spirit of Detroit has worn jerseys for championship teams, public health campaigns, and community celebrations — becoming an active participant in the city's shared triumphs.

Sports Teams
Championship celebrations for Detroit's beloved professional and collegiate athletic teams — Lions, Tigers, Pistons, Red Wings, and more.
Community Organizations
Cultural events, awareness campaigns, and neighborhood milestones — community groups can dress the Spirit to amplify their message citywide.
Government & Civic
Official ceremonies, proclamation days, and government recognition programs that honor public service and civic achievement across Detroit and Wayne County.

Getting Started

How to Request Use of the Spirit of Detroit

01
Submit Request
Complete the online request form with your organization's details, proposed dates, and the nature of your event or jersey program. All submissions are reviewed by DWJBA staff.
02
Review Period
DWJBA reviews all requests to ensure alignment with the Spirit's civic mission and community values. You can expect a response within 5–7 business days.
03
Approval & Agreement
Upon approval, DWJBA will issue a formal agreement outlining the terms of use, event logistics, installation requirements, and any applicable guidelines.
04
Execution
DWJBA coordinates with your team on installation, event staffing, and site management. Our team ensures every activation honors the dignity and significance of the landmark.
