Our Authority

Stewards of Detroit's Most Critical Public Buildings

The Detroit-Wayne Joint Building Authority is the bi-jurisdictional body responsible for managing, maintaining, and modernizing the civic infrastructure shared by the City of Detroit and Wayne County. We operate under a mandate of excellence — serving the government agencies, courts, and members of the public who rely on these buildings every day.

From 24/7 security and facilities management to capital improvement programs and sustainability initiatives, the DWJBA operates at the highest standards of public accountability.

Two Premier
Civic Facilities

At the heart of Detroit's civic operations stand two extraordinary facilities, each playing a critical role in serving the City and Wayne County. These are more than buildings — they are anchors of public service, innovation, and community trust.

The Coleman A. Young Municipal Center houses essential government operations including executive, legislative, and judicial functions, while the Spirit of Detroit Plaza stands as an iconic cultural landmark open to all Detroiters.
